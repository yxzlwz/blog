## Linear Equation (线性方程)

For $n \geq 1$, a ==linear equation== in the variables $x_1, ... , x_n$ is an equation that can be written in the form

$$ a_1x_1 + a_2x_2 + ... + a_nx_n = b $$

where $b$ and the coefficients $a_1, ... , a_n$ are real or complex numbers.

::: tip Examples

- $\sqrt{2}x_1 + \pi x_2 = 0$ is a linear equation.

- $x_1x_2 + x_3 = 0$ is NOT a linear equation because of the term $x_1x_2$.

- $x_1 + \sqrt{x_2} = 0$ is NOT a linear equation because of the term $\sqrt{x_2}$.

- $0x_1 = 0$ is a linear equation.

- $0x_1 = 1$ is a linear equation, although it has no solution.

:::

## Linear System (线性方程组)

### Definition

A ==linear system== is a collection of one or more linear equations involving the same variables (e.g., $x_1, ... , x_n$).

### Solution of Linear Systems (线性方程组的解)

- ==A solution of a linear system== is a (ordered) list
$(s_1, s_2, ... ,s_n)$ of numbers that makes each equation a true statement when the values $s_1, s_2, ... ,s_n$ are substituted for $x_1, ... , x_n$, respectively.

- The set of all possible solutions is called the ==solution set of the linear system (线性方程组的解集)==.

- A linear system may have:
  - no solution
  - exactly one solution
  - infinitely many solutions

- A linear system that has at least one solution is called ==consistent (相容的)==; otherwise, it is called ==inconsistent (不相容的)==.

### Equivalent Linear Systems (等价线性方程组)

Two linear systems are called ==equivalent== if they have the same solution set.

## Matrix Notation of Linear Systems (线性方程组的矩阵表示法)

Take the following linear system as an example:

$$
\begin{cases}
\begin{aligned}
x_1 - 2x_2 + x_3 = 0 \\
2x_2 - 8x_3 = 8 \\
5x_1 - 5x_3 = 10
\end{aligned}
\end{cases}
$$

the matrix

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & -2 & 1 \\
0 & 2  & -8 \\
5 & 0  & -5
\end{bmatrix}
$$

is called the ==coefficient matrix (系数矩阵)==, and the matrix

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & -2 & 1 & 0\\
0 & 2  & -8 & 8\\
5 & 0  & -5 & 10
\end{bmatrix}
$$

is called the ==augmented matrix (增广矩阵)==.

## Soving a Linear System with Augmented Matrix (用增广矩阵解线性方程组)

Take the following linear system as an example:

$$
\begin{cases}
\begin{aligned}
x_1 &- 2x_2 &+ x_3 &= 0 \\
3x_1 & &- 8x_3 &= 8 \\
5x_1 &+ 2x_2 &- 5x_3 &= 10
\end{aligned}
\end{cases}
$$

The corresponding augmented matrix is
$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & -2 & 1 & 0\\
3 & 0  & -8 & 8\\
5 & 2  & -5 & 10
\end{bmatrix}
$$

We can apply a sequence of elementary row operations to transform it into its RREF.

Firstly, we can eliminate the first entry in the rows below the first row:

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & -2 & 1 & 0\\
3 & 0  & -8 & 8\\
5 & 2  & -5 & 10
\end{bmatrix}
\overset{R_2 - 3R_1}{\underset{R_3 - 5R_1}{\longrightarrow}}
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & -2 & 1 & 0\\
0 & 6  & -11 & 8\\
0 & 12 & -10 & 10
\end{bmatrix}
$$

Next, we can eliminate the second entry in the third row:

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & -2 & 1 & 0\\
0 & 6  & -11 & 8\\
0 & 12 & -10 & 10
\end{bmatrix}
\overset{R_3 - 2R_2}{\longrightarrow}
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & -2 & 1 & 0\\
0 & 6  & -11 & 8\\
0 & 0  & 14 & -6
\end{bmatrix}
$$

Finally, we can scale each row to make the leading entries equal to $1$, and then eliminate the entries above each leading $1$:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & -2 & 1 & 0\\
0 & 6  & -11 & 8\\
0 & 0  & 14 & -6
\end{bmatrix}
& \overset{\frac{R_2}{6}}{\underset{\frac{R_3}{14}}{\longrightarrow}}
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & -2 & 1 & 0\\[3pt]
0 & 1  & -\frac{11}{6} & \frac{4}{3}\\[3pt]
0 & 0  & 1 & -\frac{3}{7}
\end{bmatrix}
\\
& \overset{R_2 + \frac{11}{6}R_3}{\underset{R_1 - R_3}{\longrightarrow}}
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 & 0 & \frac{3}{7}\\[3pt]
0 & 1 & 0 & \frac{23}{42}\\[3pt]
0 & 0 & 1 & -\frac{3}{7}
\end{bmatrix}
\\
& \overset{R_1 - 2R_2}{\longrightarrow}
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & 0 & -\frac{14}{21}\\[3pt]
0 & 1 & 0 & \frac{23}{42}\\[3pt]
0 & 0 & 1 & -\frac{3}{7}
\end{bmatrix}
\end{aligned}
$$

Now, according to the final RREF, we can directly read out the solution of the linear system:

$$
(x_1, x_2, x_3) = (-\frac{14}{21}, \frac{23}{42}, -\frac{3}{7})
$$

## Consistence (相容性)

A linear system is consistent if and only if the rightmost column of the augmented matrix is NOT a pivot column.

Simply put, a linear system is inconsistent if and only if the augmented matrix (in RREF) has a row of the form $\begin{bmatrix}0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & b\end{bmatrix}$, where $b$ is a nonzero number.

## Basic and Free Variables (主变量和自由变量)

Take the following linear system as an example:

$$
\begin{cases}
\begin{aligned}
x_1 & &+ x_3 &= 2 \\
x_1 &+ 2x_2 &+ x_3 &= 4
\end{aligned}
\end{cases}
$$

The corresponding augmented matrix is

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & 1 & 2\\
1 & 2 & 1 & 4
\end{bmatrix}
\longrightarrow
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & 1 & 2\\
0 & 1 & 0 & 1
\end{bmatrix}
$$

The solution of the linear system can be written as

$$
\begin{cases}
\begin{aligned}
x_1 &= 2 - x_3 \\
x_2 &= 1 \\
x_3 &\text{ is free}
\end{aligned}
\end{cases}
$$

- The variables $x_1$ and $x_2$ corresponding to the pivot columns in the matrix are called ==basic variables==.
- The other variable, $x_3$, is called a ==free variable==.

## Parametric Vector Form (参数向量形式)

Following the above example, we can express the solution set of the linear system as

$$
\begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3\end{bmatrix}
=
\begin{bmatrix}2 - x_3 \\ 1 \\ x_3\end{bmatrix}
=
\begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 1 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}
+
\begin{bmatrix}-x_3 \\ 0 \\ x_3\end{bmatrix}
=
\begin{bmatrix}2 \\ 1 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}
+
x_3\begin{bmatrix}-1 \\ 0 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}
$$

## $A\vec{x} = \vec{b}$ Notation of Linear Systems

A linear system can be written in the matrix equation form $A\vec{x} = \vec{b}$, where

- $A$ is the coefficient matrix,
- $\vec{x}$ is the column vector of the variables,
- $\vec{b}$ is the column vector of the constants on the right side of the equations.

For example, the linear system

$$
\begin{cases}
\begin{aligned}
x_1 &- 2x_2 &+ x_3 &= 0 \\
3x_1 & &- 8x_3 &= 8 \\
5x_1 &+ 2x_2 &- 5x_3 &= 10
\end{aligned}
\end{cases}
$$

can be written as

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & -2 & 1 \\
3 & 0  & -8 \\
5 & 2  & -5
\end{bmatrix}
\begin{bmatrix}
x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3
\end{bmatrix}
=
\begin{bmatrix}
0 \\ 8 \\ 10
\end{bmatrix}
$$

## Homogeneity

### Homogeneous Linear Systems

A linear system is called a ==homogeneous linear system (齐次线性方程组)== if it can be written in the form $A\vec{x} = \vec{0}$.

Obviously, a homogeneous linear system always has at least one solution $\vec{x} = \vec{0}$, which is called the ==trivial solution (平凡解)==. If there exists a solution $\vec{x} \neq \vec{0}$, we call it a ==nontrivial solution (非平凡解)==.

If a homogeneous linear system has a nontrivial solution, then it must have infinitely many solutions. This is also obvious because if $\vec{x}$ is a nontrivial solution, then for any scalar $c$, $c\vec{x}$ is also a solution.

A homogeneous linear system has a nontrivial solution if and only if there is at least one free variable. Otherwise, if every variable is basic, then there's only one solution, which is the trivial solution.

### Nonhomogeneous Linear Systems

A linear system is called a ==nonhomogeneous linear system (非齐次线性方程组)== if it can be written in the form $A\vec{x} = \vec{b}$ with $\vec{b} \neq \vec{0}$.

According to the definition of matrix–vector multiplication, we can conclude that $A\vec{x}$ has the same solution set as the vector equation $x_1\vec{v_1} + x_2\vec{v_2} + \cdots + x_n\vec{v_n} = \vec{b}$, where $\vec{v_1}, \vec{v_2}, \ldots, \vec{v_n}$ are the column vectors of $A$. The augmented matrix of the vector equation can be written as $\begin{bmatrix} \vec{v_1} & \vec{v_2} & \cdots & \vec{v_n} & \vec{b} \end{bmatrix}$.

The following statements are equivalent (either they are all true or all false):

- For each $\vec{b}$ in $\mathbb{R}^m$, the equation $A\vec{x} = \vec{b}$ has a solution.
- Each $\vec{b}$ in $\mathbb{R}^m$ is a linear combination of the columns of $A$.
- The columns of $A$ span $\mathbb{R}^m$.
- $A$ (NOT the augmented matrix) has a pivot position in every row.

Suppose $A\vec{x} = \vec{b}$ is consistent for some $\vec{b}$. Let $\vec{p}$ be a solution to $A\vec{x} = \vec{b}$. Then the solution set of $A\vec{x} = \vec{b}$ can be expressed as $\vec{x} = \vec{p} + \vec{v}$, where $\vec{v}$ is any solution of the corresponding homogeneous equation $A\vec{x} = \vec{0}$.
