2. Echelon Form and Pivot

2026-02-13

Elementary Row Operations (初等行变换)

  1. Interchange: Interchange two rows.
  2. Scaling: Multiply all entries in a row by a nonzero constant.
  3. Replacement: Replace one row by the sum of itself and a multiple of another row.

Example

Two matrices are called row equivalent (行等价) if there is a sequence of elementary row operations that transforms one matrix into the other.

Echelon Form (阶梯形)

Some Definitions

A nonzero row or column in a matrix means a row or column that contains at least one nonzero entry.

非零行或列是指至少包含一个非零元素的行或列。

A leading entry of a row refers to the leftmost nonzero entry in a nonzero row.

行的主元素是指一个非零行中最左边的非零元素。

Row Echelon Form (REF)

A rectangular matrix is in row echelon form (阶梯形) (REF) if it has the following properties:

  1. All nonzero rows are above any rows of all zeros.
    • [0243−10000]\begin{bmatrix}0 & 2 & 4 \\ 3 & -1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}​030​2−10​400​​ satisfies this property.
    • [000123045]\begin{bmatrix}0 & 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 0 & 4 & 5\end{bmatrix}​010​024​035​​ does not satisfy this property.
  2. Each leading entry of a row is in a column to the right of the leading entry of the row above it.
    • [120300450006]\begin{bmatrix}\color{cyan}{1} & 2 & 0 & 3 \\ 0 & 0 & \color{cyan}{4} & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & \color{cyan}{6}\end{bmatrix}​100​200​040​356​​ satisfies this property.
    • [023456007]\begin{bmatrix}0 & \color{cyan}{2} & 3 \\ \color{red}{4} & 5 & 6 \\ 0 & 0 & \color{cyan}{7}\end{bmatrix}​040​250​367​​ does not satisfy this property.
  3. All entries in a column below a leading entry are zeros.
    • In fact, any matrix that satisfies property 2 automatically satisfies this property.

Reduced Row Echelon Form (RREF)

If a matrix in echelon form (satisfies all three mentioned properties) satisfies the following additional conditions, then it is in reduced (row) echelon form (简化阶梯形) (RREF):

  1. The leading entry in each nonzero row is 111.
  2. Each leading 111 is the only nonzero entry in its column.
    • [1530012000010000]\begin{bmatrix}\color{cyan}{1} & \color{red}{5} & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & \color{cyan}{1} & 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & \color{cyan}{1} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}​1000​5100​3200​0010​​ does NOT satisfies this property.

Each matrix is row equivalent to one and only one reduced echelon matrix (RREF).

Achieving RREF

We can achieve the RREF of a matrix by applying a sequence of elementary row operations. Take the following matrix as an example:

[1−2130−852−5]\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -2 & 1\\ 3 & 0 & -8\\ 5 & 2 & -5 \end{bmatrix} ​135​−202​1−8−5​​

We can apply a sequence of elementary row operations to transform it into its RREF.

Firstly, we can eliminate the first entry in the rows below the first row:

Next, we can eliminate the second entry in the third row:

[1−2106−11012−10]⟶R3−2R2[1−2106−110014]\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -2 & 1\\ 0 & 6 & -11\\ 0 & 12 & -10 \end{bmatrix} \overset{R_3 - 2R_2}{\longrightarrow} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -2 & 1\\ 0 & 6 & -11\\ 0 & 0 & 14 \end{bmatrix} ​100​−2612​1−11−10​​⟶R3​−2R2​​​100​−260​1−1114​​

Finally, we can scale each row to make the leading entries equal to 111, and then eliminate the entries above each leading 111:

Now it's in RREF.

Pivot (主元)

  • A pivot position (主元位置) in a matrix AAA is a location in AAA that corresponds to a leading 111 in the reduced echelon form AAA.
  • A pivot (主元) is a nonzero number in a pivot position.
  • A pivot column (主元列) is a column of AAA that contains a pivot position.

The number of pivots in a m×nm \times nm×n matrix is at most min⁡{m,n}\min\{m, n\}min{m,n}.
