Vectors

A n×1n \times 1n×1 matrix is called a nnn dimensional column vector or simply a nnnD vector:

w⃗=[w1w2⋮wn]\vec{w} = \begin{bmatrix} w_1 \\ w_2 \\ \vdots \\ w_n \end{bmatrix} w=​w1​w2​⋮wn​​​

Sometimes, we will just call as vector if it is clear that is 2D.

Rn\mathbb{R}^nRn is the set of all nnnD vectors (向量空间) with real number entries (read r-N).

Specifically, we denote 0⃗\vec{0}0 as the zero vector (零向量):

0⃗=[00⋮0]\vec{0} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ \vdots \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} 0=​00⋮0​​

Operations on Vectors

Vector Addition

Given two vectors u⃗,v⃗∈Rn\vec{u}, \vec{v} \in \mathbb{R}^nu,v∈Rn, their sum u⃗+v⃗\vec{u} + \vec{v}u+v is defined as:

u⃗+v⃗=[u1+v1u2+v2⋮un+vn]\vec{u} + \vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} u_1 + v_1 \\ u_2 + v_2 \\ \vdots \\ u_n + v_n \end{bmatrix} u+v=​u1​+v1​u2​+v2​⋮un​+vn​​​

Scalar Multiplication

Given a vector u⃗∈Rn\vec{u} \in \mathbb{R}^nu∈Rn and a scalar c∈Rc \in \mathbb{R}c∈R, the scalar multiplication cu⃗c\vec{u}cu is defined as:

cu⃗=[cu1cu2⋮cun]c\vec{u} = \begin{bmatrix} cu_1 \\ cu_2 \\ \vdots \\ cu_n \end{bmatrix} cu=​cu1​cu2​⋮cun​​​

Linear Combinations

A linear combination (线性组合) of vectors v1⃗,v2⃗,…,vk⃗∈Rn\vec{v_1}, \vec{v_2}, \ldots, \vec{v_k} \in \mathbb{R}^nv1​​,v2​​,…,vk​​∈Rn with scalars c1,c2,…,ck∈Rc_1, c_2, \ldots, c_k \in \mathbb{R}c1​,c2​,…,ck​∈R is defined as:

c1v1⃗+c2v2⃗+…+ckvk⃗c_1\vec{v_1} + c_2\vec{v_2} + \ldots + c_k\vec{v_k} c1​v1​​+c2​v2​​+…+ck​vk​​

To determine whether a vector b⃗∈Rn\vec{b} \in \mathbb{R}^nb∈Rn can be expressed as a linear combination of vectors v1⃗,v2⃗,…,vk⃗∈Rn\vec{v_1}, \vec{v_2}, \ldots, \vec{v_k} \in \mathbb{R}^nv1​​,v2​​,…,vk​​∈Rn, we can set up the following equation:

x1v1⃗+x2v2⃗+…+xkvk⃗=b⃗x_1\vec{v_1} + x_2\vec{v_2} + \ldots + x_k\vec{v_k} = \vec{b} x1​v1​​+x2​v2​​+…+xk​vk​​=b

This equation can be rewritten in augmented matrix form:

[v1,1v2,1…vk,1b1v1,2v2,2…vk,2b2⋮⋮⋱⋮⋮v1,nv2,n…vk,nbn]\begin{bmatrix} v_{1,1} & v_{2,1} & \ldots & v_{k,1} & b_1 \\ v_{1,2} & v_{2,2} & \ldots & v_{k,2} & b_2 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots \\ v_{1,n} & v_{2,n} & \ldots & v_{k,n} & b_n \end{bmatrix} ​v1,1​v1,2​⋮v1,n​​v2,1​v2,2​⋮v2,n​​……⋱…​vk,1​vk,2​⋮vk,n​​b1​b2​⋮bn​​​

For brevity, write this matrix in a way that identifies its columns:

[v1⃗v2⃗…vk⃗b⃗]\begin{bmatrix} \vec{v_1} & \vec{v_2} & \ldots & \vec{v_k} & \vec{b} \\ \end{bmatrix} [v1​​​v2​​​…​vk​​​b​]

Span (张成空间)

If v1⃗,v2⃗,…,vk⃗∈Rn\vec{v_1}, \vec{v_2}, \ldots, \vec{v_k} \in \mathbb{R}^nv1​​,v2​​,…,vk​​∈Rn, then the set of all linear combinations of these vectors is denoted by

Span{v1⃗,v2⃗,…,vk⃗}Span\{\vec{v_1}, \vec{v_2}, \ldots, \vec{v_k}\} Span{v1​​,v2​​,…,vk​​}

and is called the subset of Rn\mathbb{R}^nRn spanned by v1⃗,v2⃗,…,vk⃗\vec{v_1}, \vec{v_2}, \ldots, \vec{v_k}v1​​,v2​​,…,vk​​.
