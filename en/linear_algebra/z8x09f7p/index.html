3. Row Reduction and Echelon Forms

2026-01-26

Some Definitions

Nonzero Row or Column

A nonzero row or column in a matrix means a row or column that contains at least one nonzero entry.

Leading Entry

A leading entry of a row refers to the leftmost nonzero entry (in a nonzero row).

Echelon Form (阶梯形)

REF and RREF

A rectangular matrix is in row echelon form (REF) if it has the following properties:

  1. All nonzero rows are above any rows of all zeros.
    • [0243−10000]\begin{bmatrix}0 & 2 & 4 \\ 3 & -1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}​030​2−10​400​​ satisfies this property.
    • [000123045]\begin{bmatrix}0 & 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 0 & 4 & 5\end{bmatrix}​010​024​035​​ does not satisfy this property.
  2. Each leading entry of a row is in a column to the right of the leading entry of the row above it.
    • [120300450006]\begin{bmatrix}\color{blue}{1} & 2 & 0 & 3 \\ 0 & 0 & \color{blue}{4} & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & \color{blue}{6}\end{bmatrix}​100​200​040​356​​ satisfies this property.
    • [023456007]\begin{bmatrix}0 & \color{blue}{2} & 3 \\ \color{red}{4} & 5 & 6 \\ 0 & 0 & \color{blue}{7}\end{bmatrix}​040​250​367​​ does not satisfy this property.
  3. All entries in a column below a leading entry are zeros.
    • In fact, any matrix that satisfies property 2 automatically satisfies this property.

If a matrix in echelon form satisfies the following additional conditions, then it is in reduced (row) echelon form (RREF):

  1. The leading entry in each nonzero row is 111.
  2. Each leading 111 is the only nonzero entry in its column.
    • [1530012000010000]\begin{bmatrix}\color{blue}{1} & \color{red}{5} & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & \color{blue}{1} & 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & \color{blue}{1} \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{bmatrix}​1000​5100​3200​0010​​ does NOT satisfies this property.

Theorem

Each matrix is row equivalent to one and only one reduced echelon matrix (RREF).

Pivot

  • A pivot position in a matrix AAA is a location in AAA that corresponds to a leading 111 in the reduced echelon form AAA.
  • A pivot is a nonzero number in a pivot position.
  • A pivot column is a column of AAA that contains a pivot position.

Solutions of Linear Systems

Basic and Free Variables

Take the following linear system as an example:

[10−5101140000]{x1−5x3=1x2+x3=40=0\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -5 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 & 4 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{cases} \begin{aligned} &x_1 & - 5&x_3 &= 1 \\ & &x_2 + &x_3 &= 4 \\ & & & 0 &= 0 \end{aligned} \end{cases} ​100​010​−510​140​​⎩⎨⎧​​x1​​−5x2​+​x3​x3​0​=1=4=0​​

  • The variables x1x_1x1​ and x2x_2x2​ corresponding to the pivot columns in the matrix are called basic variables.
  • The other variable, x3x_3x3​, is called a free variable.

It is possible to rewrite the basic variables depending on the free variables:

{x1=1+5x3x2=4−x3x3 is free\begin{cases} \begin{aligned} x_1 &= 1 + 5x_3 \\ x_2 &= 4 - x_3 \\ x_3 &\text{ is free} \end{aligned} \end{cases} ⎩⎨⎧​x1​x2​x3​​=1+5x3​=4−x3​ is free​​

Consistence

A linear system is consistent if and only if the rightmost column of the augmented matrix is NOT a pivot column.

In other words, a linear system is inconsistent if and only if the augmented matrix has a row of the form [00⋯0b]\begin{bmatrix}0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & b\end{bmatrix}[0​0​⋯​0​b​], where bbb is a nonzero number.

If a linear system is consistent, then it has either:

  • a unique solution (if there are no free variables), or
  • infinitely many solutions (if there is at least one free variable).
